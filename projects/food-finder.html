Web-Connected Smart Shelf Insert

Project Overview

This web-connected smart shelf system improves accessibility in community pantries by tracking real-time inventory. As Project Manager and Mechanical Design Engineer, I led a five-person team to integrate mechanical, electrical, and digital design goals into a functional prototype that was awarded Best in Section at the CU Engineering Fall Design Expo.

Image 1: System Overview

My Role

I led the mechanical design using CAD modeling and prototyping to develop the physical system. I collaborated with a five-person team to integrate mechanical, electrical, and digital design goals, conducting testing and iteration to improve durability, compatibility, and assembly efficiency.

Image 2: Prototype

Technical Features

  • CAD modeling and iterative prototyping
  • Integration of mechanical, electrical, and digital systems
  • Testing and iteration for improved durability and compatibility
  • Optimized for assembly efficiency and real-world deployment
Image 3: Technical Details

Outcome

Awarded Best in Section at the CU Engineering Fall Design Expo for excellence in product development, analysis, and presentation. The project successfully demonstrated the integration of cross-disciplinary engineering skills to address community accessibility challenges.
