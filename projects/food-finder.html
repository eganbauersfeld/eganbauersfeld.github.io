Food Finder — Smart Shelf Project

Fall 2024 · CU Engineering Design Expo · Best in Section Award

Food Finder is a web-connected Smart Shelf insert designed to improve accessibility and usability in community food pantries. Our team of four developed a system capable of tracking inventory levels, communicating with a database, and providing real-time usage statistics.

My Role

I led the mechanical design and development of the system, including researching sustainability challenges, generating concepts, building prototypes, and optimizing the final design. I also contributed to testing and data validation.

Key Features
  • Adjustable, drop-in shelf insert for existing pantry infrastructure
  • Load-cell-based weight sensing system
  • Web-connected database showing real-time inventory levels
  • Mechanical design optimized for manufacturability and durability
Tools & Skills
  • SolidWorks mechanical modeling
  • 3D printing and rapid prototyping
  • Hardware testing and validation
  • Systems integration
Outcome

Our project won Best in Section at the Fall 2024 CU Engineering Design Expo out of 89 teams. Food Finder will continue to be iterated on with the goal of being implemented in nearby community pantries.
