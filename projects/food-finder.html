Food Finder – Smart Shelf System

Overview

Food Finder is a web-connected smart shelf insert designed to improve accessibility and usability in community food pantries. The system measures inventory levels in real time, uploads data to a database, and helps pantry managers track product demand and availability.

My Role

I led the mechanical design and development of the system. This included:

  • Researching sustainability and community pantry needs
  • Concept generation and mechanical prototyping
  • 3D modeling and fabrication
  • Hardware integration and system optimization
  • Testing and validating the final design

Key Features

  • Drop-in shelf insert compatible with existing pantry shelving
  • Load-cell-based weight sensing for real-time inventory tracking
  • Cloud-connected database for remote monitoring
  • Durable, manufacturable mechanical framework

Technical Tools

  • SolidWorks (mechanical CAD)
  • 3D printing and rapid prototyping
  • Electronics integration (load cells + microcontrollers)
  • Systems engineering + testing

Outcome

Our team received Best in Section at the Fall 2024 CU Engineering Design Expo, selected from 89 teams and over 500 total students. Food Finder continues to be refined with the goal of implementation in nearby community food-pantries.
