Truss Design Project

Overview

The goal of this project was to design, analyze, and test a 22 inch truss capable of supporting an 800-lb load while focusing on aesthetics and minimizing weight. By utilizing materials testing devices, MATLAB, and Excel, and fundamental principles from Statics, our team created a truss that far exceeded the design load. The project emphasized how design decisions influence load paths and stress distribution in real-world structures.

My Role

I led the structural layout and member sizing, built the analytical model, and supported physical construction and testing. I focused on combining aesthetic geometry with efficient load paths to minimize weight while maintaining sufficient safety factors.

Analysis

  • MATLAB tension/compression modeling
  • Factor of safety calculations
  • Load path evaluation
  • Member buckling analysis

Design

  • Hand-sketched truss concepts
  • Iterative SolidWorks modeling
  • Weight optimization
  • Pentagonal geometry for aesthetics & efficiency

Manufacturing

  • Balsa wood laser cutting
  • Precision sanding & trimming
  • High-strength adhesive application
  • Scale-accurate prototype assembly

Outcome

The truss successfully supported loads well above the design target before failure. The project reinforced my understanding of how theoretical Statics principles connect to real-world structures and gave me hands-on experience moving from analysis to fabrication and testing.
