Truss Design Project

Overview

The goal of this project was to design, analyze, and test a 22-inch truss capable of supporting an 800-lb load while minimizing weight and maintaining structural efficiency. Using MATLAB, Excel, materials testing data, and principles from Statics, we engineered a truss that exceeded expectations.

My Role

I led structural analysis, member selection, and overall geometric layout. I modeled load paths, optimized member sizing, and helped build and test the final truss structure.

Analysis

  • MATLAB tension/compression modeling
  • Factor of safety calculations
  • Member buckling analysis
  • Load path evaluation

Design

  • Hand-sketched truss concepts
  • SolidWorks iterative geometry refinement
  • Member weight optimization
  • Pentagonal frame layout

Manufacturing

  • Balsa wood laser cutting
  • Precision sanding & trimming
  • High-strength adhesive bonding
  • Scaled prototype assembly

Outcome

The truss carried a load far exceeding the design requirement before failure. This project strengthened my understanding of structural behavior and demonstrated how analytical design translates to physical performance.
