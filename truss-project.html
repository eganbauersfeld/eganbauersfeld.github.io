Truss Design Project

Overview

The goal of this project was to design, analyze, and test a 22 inch truss capable of supporting an 800-lb load while focusing on aesthetics and minimizing weight. By utilizing materials testing devices, MATLAB, and Excel, and fundamental principles from Statics, we designed a truss that far exceeded the . The project emphasized understanding how design decisions influence load paths and stress distribution in real-world structures.

My Role

I led the mechanical design: CAD modeling, prototyping, rapid iteration, hardware integration, and final optimization. I also supported electronics mounting and system-level testing.

Analysis

  • MATLAB tension/compression modeling
  • Factor of safety calculations
  • Load path evaluation
  • Member buckling analysis

Design

  • Hand-sketched truss concepts
  • Iterative SolidWorks modeling
  • Weight optimization
  • Pentagonal geometry for aesthetics & efficiency

Manufacturing

  • Balsa wood laser cutting
  • Precision sanding & trimming
  • High-strength adhesive application
  • Scale-accurate prototype assembly
